Scientific Evidence for Alpilean Weight Loss Support  

Zach Miller the creator of the Alpilean team point to recent research — a 2022 study — to validate their claims about body temperature and weight loss. However, previous studies have also linked weight loss and body temperature.

In this 2009 study, for example, researchers found that obese people tended to have significantly lower body temperatures than thinner people.

The researchers in that study also found that the difference in body temperature led to worse metabolic efficiency or slower metabolism. Obese people tended to burn fewer calories than leaner people.

To test the theory, researchers in the study gave a group of people an extra 1,000 calories a day. Researchers found that the thinnest people naturally burned 60% of their excess calories. Meanwhile, overweight people burned off "almost none" of their excess calories.

The researchers attributed the difference to the temperature difference: Thinner people tended to distribute excess calories as heat, expelling the excess calories from their bodies and burning off the energy immediately. Because obese people had lower body temperatures, they weren't able to burn off excess calories as heat; instead, their bodies stored the extra calories faster.
